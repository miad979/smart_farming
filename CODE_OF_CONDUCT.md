# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in the Smart Farming Management System community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community focused on empowering farmers in Bangladesh through technology.

---

## Our Standards

### Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

* **Being respectful** of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
* **Giving and gracefully accepting** constructive feedback
* **Accepting responsibility** and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, and learning from the experience
* **Focusing on what is best** not just for us as individuals, but for the overall community
* **Showing empathy** towards other community members
* **Supporting farmers** and their needs as our primary mission
* **Welcoming newcomers** and helping them get started
* **Celebrating successes** of others in the community
* **Being patient** with those learning or contributing for the first time

### Examples of unacceptable behavior:

* The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind
* Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
* Public or private harassment
* Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address, without their explicit permission
* Dismissing or attacking inclusion-oriented requests
* Sustained disruption of community discussions
* Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting
* Deliberate intimidation or stalking
* Unwelcome following or unwelcome attention
* Pattern of inappropriate social contact, such as requesting/assuming inappropriate levels of intimacy

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:

* GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions)
* Project documentation
* Project website and forums
* Social media accounts affiliated with the project
* Email communications
* Virtual or in-person events organized by the project

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Reporting Guidelines

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please report it by:

**Email:** conduct@smartfarming.bd

**Include in your report:**
* Your contact information (optional if you wish to remain anonymous)
* Names (real, nicknames, or pseudonyms) of any individuals involved
* Description of the incident
* When and where it occurred
* Additional context or information
* If you believe this is ongoing

### What to expect:

1. **Acknowledgment** within 24-48 hours
2. **Investigation** of the incident
3. **Decision** on appropriate action
4. **Follow-up** with reporter
5. **Action** taken against violators (if necessary)

### Confidentiality

All reports will be kept confidential. Details will only be shared with:
* The Code of Conduct response team
* Those directly involved in resolving the incident

---

## Enforcement

### Process

1. **Report received** → Acknowledged within 48 hours
2. **Investigation** → Gather information from all parties
3. **Determination** → Decide if Code of Conduct was violated
4. **Action** → Take appropriate corrective action
5. **Follow-up** → Check in with affected parties

### Possible Actions

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining consequences:

#### 1. Correction

**Community Impact:** Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome.

**Consequence:** A private, written warning providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

#### 2. Warning

**Community Impact:** A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence:** A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

#### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact:** A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence:** A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

#### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact:** Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence:** A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

## Appeals

If you feel you have been unfairly accused of violating this Code of Conduct, you may appeal the decision by:

1. **Submitting an appeal** to appeals@smartfarming.bd within 14 days
2. **Include** in your appeal:
   * Original incident report reference
   * Reason for appeal
   * New information or context
3. **Response** will be provided within 30 days
4. **Final decision** will be made by a separate review panel

## Community Guidelines

### Participation

* **Be welcoming:** Help newcomers feel comfortable
* **Be patient:** Everyone is learning
* **Be thoughtful:** Your work affects farmers' livelihoods
* **Be respectful:** Disagreement is not an opportunity to attack
* **Be charitable:** Assume good intentions
* **Be constructive:** Offer solutions, not just criticism

### Communication

* **Ask questions** when you don't understand
* **Provide context** when seeking help
* **Be clear and concise** in your communications
* **Use appropriate channels** for different types of discussions
* **Respect others' time** by being prepared and organized
* **Stay on topic** in discussions

### Collaboration

* **Give credit** where credit is due
* **Review code constructively** and respectfully
* **Accept feedback gracefully** and learn from it
* **Prioritize impact** on farmers and agricultural experts
* **Document your work** for others to understand
* **Test thoroughly** before submitting

## Cultural Sensitivity

### Working with the Bangladeshi Farming Community

This project serves Bangladeshi farmers. Please be mindful:

* **Language:** Primary language is Bengali (বাংলা)
* **Literacy levels:** Design for varying literacy levels
* **Cultural context:** Understand local farming practices
* **Respect traditions:** Agricultural traditions are important
* **Economic sensitivity:** Many users face economic challenges
* **Inclusivity:** Ensure features work in low-resource settings

### Communication Norms

* **Respect time zones:** Contributors may be global
* **Be patient with language barriers:** English may not be everyone's first language
* **Avoid idioms or slang:** May not translate well
* **Provide both Bengali and English:** When possible
* **Be explicit:** Don't assume cultural context

## Specific Scenarios

### Technical Disagreements

**DO:**
* Focus on ideas, not people
* Provide reasoning and evidence
* Be open to other viewpoints
* Seek consensus
* Escalate to maintainers if needed

**DON'T:**
* Make personal attacks
* Dismiss others' expertise
* Be inflexible or dogmatic
* Continue unproductive arguments

### Feature Requests

**DO:**
* Explain the problem you're solving
* Consider impact on farmers
* Be open to alternative solutions
* Provide use cases

**DON'T:**
* Demand features
* Criticize existing work
* Make assumptions about priorities

### Bug Reports

**DO:**
* Provide clear reproduction steps
* Be patient waiting for fixes
* Offer to help if you can
* Follow up politely

**DON'T:**
* Be demanding or rude
* Spam with duplicate issues
* Criticize maintainers
